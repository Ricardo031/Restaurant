Where It All Began

The Famiglia Rossi

In 1987, Nonno Carlo Rossi opened a small trattoria on a narrow cobblestone street in Trastevere, Rome. Armed with his mother's handwritten recipe book and a commitment to sourcing only from local farms, he built a place where neighbours became family and every meal was a reason to linger.

Today his grandchildren carry that same spirit across the ocean, preserving every technique — from hand-rolling pasta to slow-simmering the ragù for six hours — because shortcuts have no place in a kitchen built on love.

The Heart of the Kitchen

Meet Chef Sofia

Chef Sofia Rossi trained under three Michelin-starred chefs in Milan before returning to her family roots. Her philosophy is simple: respect the ingredient, respect the tradition, and never stop learning from the land.

"Every time I roll pasta by hand I feel my grandmother's hands guiding mine," she says. "That connection is what makes this food alive."
